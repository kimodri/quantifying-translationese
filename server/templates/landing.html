Quantifying
Translationese

Machine-translated datasets are commonly used to support NLP research for low-resource languages such as Filipino, but machine translation (MT) outputs frequently exhibit translationese artifacts that diverge from native usage. In Filipino, this distortion often appears as an overproduction of the pragmatically marked, subject-initial Di-Karaniwang Ayos (DKA) in place of the unmarked, verb-initial Karaniwang Ayos (KA). We present the first large-scale, multi-system empirical study quantifying MT-induced word-order bias in Filipino benchmarks and assessing its impact on evaluation validity.
